In the year 2034...
Ken Castle (Micheal C. Hall) invents self-replicating "nanites" that replace brain tissue and allow humans to control other humans' actions. After creating Society, a life sim, using this technology, Castle then creates the third-person shooter game Slayers. These two games make Castle the richest man in the world. While Society sees players control live actors, Slayers has them control death-row prisoners using real weapons in specified zones. One such Slayer is John "Kable" Tillman (Gerald Butler), who has now survived a record 27 matches. With 30 wins, Kable will walk free. He has other plans...
The Hero's Journey
Kable, using a mod developed by Humanz group, hacks out of his player's control and escapes into the real world. Friggin sweet!
Ken Castle is very awesome and very evil also. His layered schemes push Kable and company to their limits as Castle approaches world domination.
This movie portrays the 'gamer' archetype in various lights. I was blown away to see another waffle-dipping gamer such as myself.
Kable's story is one of extremes, like the extreme guy that he is. We could learn a lot from Kable, from his coolness to his more emotional moments.
